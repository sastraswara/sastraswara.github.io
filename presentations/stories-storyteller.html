Who is the Story Teller?

Dalang

  • Wedha & Wulang
  • Vedic teaching
  • Angudal piwulang
  • To narrate a teaching

Duty (Among Other)

  • Kawi radya: Master the art of storytelling
  • Sabet: Master the art of puppet movement
  • Amardibasa: Master the language of shadow puppetry
  • Parama sastra: Master the literature of shadow puppetry
  • Awicarita: Master the story being told
  • Amardawalagu: Master the art of the music

Ethic

  • Duty towards the society
  • Able to face various situations
  • Responsible
  • Decisive
  • Free from prejudice

Prohibition

  • To change substance of the story
  • To run out of story before the proper time
  • To run out of time
  • Restless during performance
  • Improper conduct

Narrative Structure

Time Division

  • Pathet Nem: 21.00—24.00
  • Pathet Sanga: 24.00—04.00
  • Pathet Manyura: 04.00—06.00

Narrative Structure

  • Roughly 3-part or 5-part structures
  • Follow a general formal scene requirements
  • Differ depending on the story / episode

Pathet Nem

  • Exposition scene (Jejer)
  • Guest
  • Kingdom scene (Kedaton)
  • Oversea scene (Sabrang)
  • Failed war scene (Perang gagal)

Pathet Sanga

  • Nature narrative (Gara-gara)
  • Servant scene (Punakawan)
  • Exposition scene
  • War scene (Perang Kembang)

Pathet Manyura

  • Exposition scene
  • Final war scene (Perang Brubuh)
  • Victory dance scene (Tayung)
  • Final exposition (Jejer Manyura)
  • Closing scene (Tancep Kayon)

Ramayana

  • Based on the work of the sage Walmiki
  • The story of Rama in his effort to get back his wife Shinta from the king giant Rahwana
  • Different versions between the Indian & Javanese version

The Story

  • Rama of Ayodya Kingdom won a competition to marry Dewi Shinta of Mantili
  • Rahwana of Alengka desires Shinta, kidnapping her
  • Hanoman the monkey, Rama's employ, tried to save Shinta
  • Rama attacks Alengka & kill Rahwana
  • Shinta proofs her purity on a trial of fire
  • Both are united

Mahabharata

  • Based on the work of the sage Wyasa
  • Great war of the two families—Pandawa & Kurawa
  • Both are descendant of the King Bharata
  • Takes place centuries after Ramayana
  • The Javanese version imbued with the teaching of Wali Songo

The Story

  • The Pandawa & Kurawa are cousins, competing for claim of the throne
  • To avoid war, the kingdom was divided into two
  • The Pandawa won a competition with Drupadi as the price
  • A cheated dice game made Kurawa gain the whole kingdom

The Story (Cont'd)

  • The Pandawa was banished for 12 years, in hiding for 1 year
  • Kurawa refused to return the kingdom
  • The Pandawa wage war against Kurawa, winning
  • The Pandawa reign over the whole kingdom

Panji

  • Contains many variations of a theme
  • Two lovers (Panji Inu Kertapati & Dewi Sekartaji) are trying to find way to each other
  • Both characters changed name & identity throughout their journey
  • Meeting, helping people, wage war along the journey
  • Considered an original story from Java
  • Example: Panji Semirang, Panji Angreni

Panji Angreni

  • Panji Inu Kertapati got married with Dewi Angreni
  • He's actually engaged as a child to Dewi Sekartaji from the Panjalu Kingdom
  • Angreni is assasinated so the engagement proceed
  • Inu Kertapati fell to amnesia, embarked as Klana Jayengsari on war campaigns

Panji Angreni (Cont'd)

  • Sekartaji went on a journey to help him, as a man
  • Both meet on the battle field
  • It is discovered, that both Sekartaji & Angreni contained the incarnated soul of Kamaratih
  • Inu Kertapati regain his memory, got married with Sekartaji
