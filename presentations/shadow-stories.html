Origin Stories

Differ on when the Wayang Kulit come into usage.

  • 1st story: during Kahuripan Kingdom (11th century)
  • 2nd story: during Demak Kingdom (15th-16th century)

Different sources:

  • 1st Story: e.g. Kakawin Arjuna Wiwaha
  • 2nd Story: e.g. Serat Sastramiruda

Description of a performance in Arjuna Wiwaha

  • A person was crying, moved by a performance
  • Even though the person knew, that it's just a moving leather

Serat Sastramiruda

  • Composed during the time of the King Paku Buwana IV (1863—1983)
  • Contain a dialogue between the teacher (Prince Arya Kusumadilaga) and the student (Mas Sastramiruda)
  • Lengthy discussion on proper practice of shadow puppetry for people of the court
  • Wayang Kulit is created during the Demak kingdom period

Development of the Wayang

  • According to Serat Sastramiruda
  • Here, only on significant forms

Wayang Purwa

  • Created by King Jayabaya (1135—1159 CE) of Kediri Kingdom
  • 896 Saka / 947 CE
  • Chronogram: Gambare Wayang Wolu
  • Pictures of ancestors
  • Drawn on lontar leaves (palm-leaf)
  • Wayang form supposedly like relief on temples

Wayang Beber

  • Created by Prabu Bratana of Majapahit Kingdom (1292—1527)
  • 1283 Saka / 1361 CE
  • Chronogram: Gunaning pujangga nembah ing dewa
  • Stories are drawn on scrolls
  • Performed as cleansing/healing ritual (Murwakala)

Remeng Mangunjaya

Wayang Kulit

  • Created during the time of Sri Sultan Syah Alam Akbar (1475—1518) of Demak Kingdom
  • 1440 Saka / 1518 CE
  • Chronogram: Sirna suci caturing dewa
  • Wayang are made with leathers
  • Changing the form due to consideration on Islamic law
  • Led to various innovations by the Nine Saints (Wali Songo)

Short Detour: Religion

  • Ancient religion, e.g. Kapitayan
  • Hinduism & Buddhism
  • Islam (Nusantara)
  • Javanese spiritualism

Kapitayan

  • The idea of Sang Hyang Taya
  • The One Great Void
  • Cannot be perceived by any senses
  • Manifests both in good and evil
  • Can only be felt (Rasa)
  • Mythical figure Danghyang Semar

Hinduism & Buddhism

  • Mahayana Buddhism & Hinduism, mainly Shaivism
  • Both came in around the 1st century
  • Becomes religion of the kingdom
  • Influences the art & literatures
  • Mixed into one during Majapahit time
  • Kakawin Sutasoma from Empu Tantular (1365 CE)
  • Even when it appears in various forms, its essence is one. For there is no duality in truth

Javanese Spiritualism

  • The teaching is expressed often in proverb, maxims, & (song)poems
  • To ask the origin and the destination of human…
  • Life is but to stop shortly for a drink
  • Rich withouth possessions, glorious without weapons, winning without defeating

Sekar Macapat Asmarandana

The Nine Saints
Wali Songo

  • Islam has been known in Java since 7th century
  • Only be spread & practiced in 15th century
  • A network of religious mission
  • Each saint has the tittle sunan, some are tied to ruling kingdom or nobles themselves
  • Adapted Islam with local cultures
  • Appeal to general people in society
  • Each of the saint has specific task or area of expertise

The Decree of the Sultan of Demak

  • Wayang tradition needs to be continued with adaptations
  • Wayang can be used as a medium for dakwah
  • The wayang form needs to be adapted
  • The stories related to god figures needs to be adapted
  • Wayang stories must contain ethical & spiritual teaching

The Decree…(cont'd)

  • The Wayang stories coming from India must be adapted
  • Wayang figures must be seen as symbols with certain interpretation
  • Wayang performance must be ethical, proper, and far from improper conduct
  • All meaning making must be thought of, including all elements related to Wayang (music instruments, composition, etc.)

Sunan Giri

  • Ruler of the independent hill area Giri
  • Descendant of Majapahit noble
  • King on the Mountain
  • Composing song-poems
  • Designing ornaments on Wayang puppets
  • Adding characters of wanara type
  • Composing stories and its songs (suluk)

Sunan Bonang

  • Son of Sunan Ampel, related to Majapahit nobility
  • Reside in the village of Bonang within Demak kingdom, then to Lasem
  • Responsible for the art of suluk & gamelan
  • Together with Sunan Kalijaga, creating tools to support a Wayang performance
  • Created ricikan puppets, e.g. animals, vehicles, etc.
  • Perform as a puppet master himself
  • A virtuoso of Bonang instrument, making technical inventions & composition

Sunan Kalijaga

  • Son of a regent in Tuban
  • A rogue in the forest of Jatisari (Lokajaya)
  • Converted to Islam after failing to steal from Sunan Bonang
  • The one who guards by the river
  • Became the traveling saint, hiding his identity

Contribution to Wayang Art

  • Redesigning the Wayang form and the mechanic thereof
  • Composing Wayang stories, most famous: Wisanggeni, Dewa Ruci
  • Together with Sunan Bonang, creating tools to support a Wayang performance
  • Wayang and dance performance as medium for dakwah
  • Reintroducing figures from Kapitayan religion
  • Composing song poems
